How we choose our picks

We do not personally test pans in a lab. Instead, we rely on three sources: long-term owner reviews from sites like Amazon and Reddit (focusing on feedback after six months or more of use), published roundups from trusted consumer magazines and baking blogs, and our own qualitative assessment of materials and construction based on widely available specifications. We prioritize pans that show consistent performance across many users rather than a single glowing review. Key failure points we look for: warping at high temperatures, nonstick coatings that peel or bubble, uneven heat distribution that leads to burnt edges, and handles that snap off. A pan that survives years of heavy use without any of these problems earns a spot on our list. We also consider ergonomics – a pan should be easy to grip even with oven mitts – and how well the pan resists scratches and stains over time.
